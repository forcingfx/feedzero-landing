FeedZero

A keyboard-driven, fast, and privacy-first RSS reader.

Open the app · Source code

About

FeedZero is a free and open source RSS reader. It runs entirely in your browser — there is nothing to install and no account to create. Your subscriptions are stored locally on your device.

It is currently in alpha (v0.2.0), so expect rough edges.

Features

  • Formats

    RSS, Atom, and JSON Feed.

    Paste a URL and FeedZero figures out the rest.

    example.com/feed.xml
    RSS Atom JSON Feed
  • Keyboard

    Everything on the keyboard.

    Vim-style navigation throughout. Press ? in the app for the full list.

    jnext /search kprev oopen rrefresh ?help
  • Subscriptions

    Bring your feeds in. Take them out.

    OPML import and export — move in from another reader, or leave without losing anything.

    XML ↔ OPML
    Feedly Inoreader
    NewsBlur Miniflux
  • Sync

    Encrypted end-to-end. Optional.

    AES-256-GCM on your device, with a four-word passphrase from the EFF wordlist — only you have the key.

    oak sun fox bell
    local → synced
  • Discover

    Around 1,000 curated feeds.

    The Explore tab organizes them by topic and country, so you have somewhere to start.

    1,000+ topics · countries
  • Reader

    Full article text, even from summaries.

    Extracts the readable body for feeds that only publish the first paragraph.

    →
    summary → full text

Privacy

There are no accounts, no tracking scripts, and no analytics. Your subscription list and read history live in your browser. If you turn on sync, your data is encrypted on your device before it is sent to the server, using a passphrase only you know.

Open source

The source code lives on GitLab. You can read it, audit it, run it yourself, or send a patch. Security notes are in SECURITY.md.

To try it, visit my.feedzero.app, paste a feed URL, and start reading. There is no sign-up step.
